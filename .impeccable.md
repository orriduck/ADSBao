## Design Context

### Users
ADSBao serves people who want a polished consumer flight-tracking experience: airport search, airport traffic browsing, and specific aircraft/callsign tracking. Users should be able to move between airport context, live map data, aircraft previews, weather, routes, and flight detail states without feeling like they are operating a professional cockpit console.

### Brand Personality
Premium, calm, and modern. The app should feel like a refined travel/transport product with enough aviation specificity to be credible, but not like a dense power-user operations terminal.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use Uber-inspired typography: geometric sans, sentence-case headings, tight line heights, neutral tracking, strong weight hierarchy, and readable body text. Use Spotify-inspired interface geometry: rounded cards, pill controls, circular icon buttons, persistent app-shell rhythm, dense but approachable browsing panels, and tactile surfaces. Both light and dark themes must be first-class. Do not copy Spotify's green/dark-only palette or Uber's full black-and-white marketing system.

### Design Principles
- Preserve all existing workflows and behavior; the change is a whole-app visual system redesign.
- Treat light and dark themes as equal products with shared geometry and typography.
- Use rounded Spotify-like components for panels, controls, chips, and navigation while avoiding generic card clutter.
- Use Uber-like type for clarity and confidence: sentence case, no decorative tracking, tight but readable hierarchy.
- Include flight-specific surfaces in the design and verification: `/aircraft/[callsign]`, selected-aircraft preview/trace, route feedback, lost-signal states, and map/sidebar behavior.
